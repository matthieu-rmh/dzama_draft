The Story

In 1980, on the remote islands of Madagascar, Lucien Fohine founded Dzama Rhum with a vision that transcended mere production. He sought to capture the very essence of the terroir, creating a liquid narrative of the island's unique botanical wealth.

What began as a local pursuit in the northern reaches of the island soon evolved into a standard-bearer for premium spirits globally. Lucien's obsession with quality meant that every batch had to reflect the specific conditions of its birth, from the sugarcane grown in volcanic earth to the aromatic influences of wild ylang-ylang.

The Terroir

Nosy Boraha: Where volcanic minerals meet the tropical trade winds.

Volcanic Foundations

The nutrient-dense volcanic soil of Madagascar imparts a distinct mineral complexity to our sugarcane, a profile found nowhere else on earth.

Island Microclimate

Humid, salted air that breathes into the aging casks.

Traditional Craft

01

Double Distillation

Our spirit is born in small-batch copper pot stills. This double distillation process ensures only the purest "heart" of the rhum is retained, concentrating the aromatic esters of the island.

02

Oak Cask Maturation

We age our reserves in Limousin oak barrels. The interaction between the spirit and the wood, accelerated by the tropical humidity, creates a deep, multi-layered profile of vanilla and spice.
